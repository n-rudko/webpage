Dr. Noah Rudko

Postdoctoral Fellow, Biological and Agricultural Engineering Department, University of Arkansas

Dr. Noah Rudko is an agricultural and biological engineer focused on surface hydrology and water quality in agricultural regions. As a Postdoctoral Fellow at the University of Arkansas, his work focuses on developing insight into transport processes of nutrients and emerging contaminants such as PFAS in both urban and agricultural systems to recommend appropriate management strategies.

He earned his PhD in 2024 from Purdue University, where his work highlighted the hydroclimatic variables that influence nutrient sources and pathways in tile-drained watersheds. He specializes in designing and implementing monitoring studies and leveraging geospatial data to improve water quality models and conservation practice implementation.

Current Research & Professional Experience

Postdoctoral Fellow (08/2024 - Present)

University of Arkansas

  • Designed and implemented PFAS monitoring of urban streams in NW Arkansas to quantify influence of sampling methods on concentration.
  • Developing a quantitative database of PFAS in agricultural systems (soil, water, plants, livestock) to inform mitigation strategies.
  • Identifying geospatial variables that predict farmer management to improve implementation of conservation practices in water quality models.

Graduate Research Assistant (08/2021 - 08/2024)

Purdue University

  • Quantified physical and biogeochemical processes influencing watershed-scale nutrient transport.
  • Synthesized data from global studies of water quality monitoring studies that quantify agricultural BMP effectiveness.
  • Led stream sampling and nutrient monitoring across multiple agricultural watersheds.

Graduate Research Assistant (06/2019 - 05/2021)

Arizona State University

  • Developed a hydrological and groundwater quality model of a Concentrated Animal Feeding Operation (CAFO) manure lagoon to determine parameters affecting pollutant fate and transport.
